Incident Response Plan
Document No. MO10 | Revision Date: Jan 2, 2025 | Author: Suraj Pandey
Approved by ISO 27001 Annex A: A.16.1.1 through A.16.1.7

1. Policy Overview
This Security Incident Response Policy is formulated in alignment with ISO 27001:2013 and is designed to establish comprehensive measures for the detection, management, and resolution of security vulnerabilities and incidents.

2. Scope
This Policy applies to all individuals who access, use, or interact with information systems managed by miniOrange, including employees, contractors, and external parties.

3. Roles and Responsibilities
The Information Security Officer and senior management team shall be responsible for implementation, maintenance, and oversight of this Policy.

4. Policy
Severity levels:
a. Highest Severity — Level One: Service failure due to environmental threat (natural disasters, terrorist attack, power failure, fire).
b. Medium Severity — Level Two: Service failure depending on third-party suppliers/vendors (AWS Downtime, Google Server Downtime).
c. Low Severity — Level Three: Service unavailable due to incidents in internal network.

Incident types include unauthorized loss/alteration/destruction of data, unauthorized modification to information processing facility, external attacks (phishing, DDoS, malware), and unexpected device malfunction.

Incident Logging
All incidents must be logged before executing the incident response plan. Employees must report any level of incident to top management/concerned team within 24 hours. Customers can report incidents to miniOrange anytime.

5. Communication Plan
Customers will be notified within 8 hrs of the incident or depending on the severity of the incident.

6. Incident Management
Phase 1: Immediate Action — ISMS Team assesses severity.
Phase 2: Testing and Monitoring — document all actions post-incident.
Phase 3: Backup Execution and Post-Incident Meeting — develop follow-up action plan.
Phase 4: Ongoing Assessment — continuous assessment until resolved.

7. Service Plan
miniOrange relies on AWS infrastructure. Incidents in AWS environment addressed per AWS SLA. Google Workspace used for internal communication; telephonic channels as contingency.

8. Generic Plan
Incident Manager empowered to take any action necessary to resolve incident.

9. Responsibilities of Incident Manager
Ensure effective communication internally and externally. Assemble response team. Oversee post-incident report.

10. Records and Reporting
ISMS Team prepares incident report with time of impact, detection, resolution, and lessons learned.

11. Review of Information Security Continuity
Following resolution, Incident Response Plan reviewed to identify deficiencies and implement corrective actions.
